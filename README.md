# Kryptonite-KryptoLok-Checkpoint-ALR-5-Mount
A space saving way to mount the Kryptonite KryptoLok to a Gen 1 Checkpoint ALR 5 which still allows for 2 water bottle holders to be mounted and utilized within the front triangle of the bike. This design doesn't interfere with the natural pedal stroke and works extremely well in practice.

This mount works flawlessly on my 2022 Checkpoint ALR 5 in a size 54cm. While your mileage may vary with different sizes of this bike, I would expect this mount to work with the same model of checkpoint in other sizes. This mount only utilizes a couple 3d printed parts, some standard hardware, and a few loose ods and ends you could find at home to go together. Supports are needed and recomended for the bike lock seat stays mount. All other parts should print fine with a 0.4mm nozzle using a 0.2mm layer height when oriented properly on their bottom surfaces. I would recomend printing the parts out of ASA or something that can handle a range of temperature and specifically something that is UV resistant. I'm sure certain PETG blends would work just fine, but they may fatigue over time.

3D Printed Parts:
1. Seat Stays Mount - holds the part of the bend on the top of the U-lock - qty 1
2. Seat Stay Mount 1 - mounts to one of the seat stays to hold the U-lock part of the lock in place - qty 1
3. Seat Stay Mount 2 - mounts to other one of the seat stays to hold the U-lock part of the lock in place - qty 1
4. Down Tube Mount - Holds the locking mechanism to the down tube of the bike - qty 1

Hardware Needed:
1. M5x12mm SHCS - qty 4
2. Small zip ties - qty 4
3. Small amount of electrical tape
4. A couple of elastic bands or hair ties
5. A short velcro tie

Instructions:
1. 3D print all printed parts and remove any supports used in printing
2. Screw the down tube mount to the highest up two mounting holes on the down tube using two of the M5x12mm SHCS
3. Add a couple of elastic bands on to the two nubs of the down tube mount and twist them over a couple of times so that they firmly secured and add some damping for the locking mechanism to sit on
4. Attach the seat stays mount using the other two M5x12mm SHCS to the mounting holes found where the seat stays meet the seat tube. The opening in the hook should be facing the top of the bike so that the U-lock sits in the crevice and the arms of the U-lock roughly follow their respective seat stays
5. Line up the two seat stays mount with the end of the U-lock. These parts should fit onto the seat stays. If they don't fit on, change which side the seat stay mount is on. Wrap a layer of electrical tape around the seat stay where the seat stay mount is going to sit, and then attach each seat stay mount to its respective location using two zip ties per mount.
6. The U shaped part of the lock can now sit in the two seat stay mounts with the top resting at the seat stays mount near the seat tube of the bike. A small velcro tie can be used to secure the bend in the u-lock to the seat stays mount. Finally the locking mechanism of the U-lock can be put onto the down tube mount and the key can be used to lock that part of the lock to the mount.
7. Now both parts of the lock are securely attach and out of the way and you can get back to riding
